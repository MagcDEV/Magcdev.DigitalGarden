 What happened in Argentina and Venezuela in the last 20 years?, well quite a lot, long story short, both countries suffered from the effects of what is called "Macroeconomic Populism" that basically can be defined as the application of populist macroeconomics policies this is the explication of the current circumstances of both countries and the main reason for the political and economic problems that are happening today, this post is an exploration of this idea and a summary of the origin and explanation of the current setting.

#### What is Macroeconomic Populism?

Macroeconomic populism, an approach to economics that prioritizes **rapid growth and income redistribution, often at the expense of fiscal prudence and external balance**, has been a recurring phenomenon in Latin America. The seminal work of Rudiger Dornbusch and Sebastian Edwards, "The Macroeconomics of Populism in Latin America," published nearly three decades ago, remains a critical framework for understanding the dynamics and consequences of such policies . Their analysis posits that while populist leaders are often motivated by a desire to address social injustices, their policy prescriptions, characterized by expansionary spending and a disregard for potential inflationary pressures and external constraints, invariably lead to economic crises that ultimately harm the very populations they intend to benefit . The historical recurrence of these episodes in Latin America, despite a well-documented history of failures, underscores the need to examine the underlying conditions and motivations that drive policymakers toward populist strategies . Understanding the patterns identified by Dornbusch and Edwards can offer valuable insights into the economic challenges faced by countries grappling with similar political and economic decisions today . This report aims to summarize the core tenets of Dornbusch and Edwards' theory and analyze its relevance to the recent economic histories of Argentina and Venezuela from 2004 to the present, a period marked by significant economic volatility in both nations.
